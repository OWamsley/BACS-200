Yoyo Styles

There are five main styles of yoyoing tricks will fall under. Understanding these categories will give you a good understanding of what can be done with yoyos, and it will make it easier for you to find videos that you will find entertaining.

1A: String Tricks

This is the style that most yoyoers start with, and it is what most people will think of when you talk about yoyos. Yoyoers often use unresponsive yoyos, allowing them to do complicated string tricks.

Learn 1A Tricks »



2A: Two-Handed Looping Tricks

This style of yoyoing uses two looping yoyos, one in each hand. Yoyos used in this style are responsive, and are shaped more like the classic yoyos many people think of.

Learn 2A Tricks »



3A: String Tricks with Two Yoyos

This style is similar to 1A, but instead, the yoyoer has a yoyo in each hand. Arguably the most difficult style of play, 3A is recommended to those with a solid base in 1A tricks first.

Learn 3A Tricks »



4A: Offstring

In this style of yoyoing, the yoyo is attached to the finger, but not to the yoyo. This unique style of yoyoing uses unique large yoyos, and is surprisingly not too difficult to learn for those with a little prior yoyo experience.

Learn 4A Tricks »



5A: Freehand

In 5A yoyoing, the string is attached to the yoyo, but it is not attached to the player's finger. It is attached to another object, often a small ball or a die. Experienced players can move both the counter weight, and the yoyo.
